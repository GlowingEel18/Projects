The View from the Cheap Seats

by Neil Gaiman

Overview

The View from the Cheap Seats is a collection of non-fiction writings by Neil Gaiman. It gathers essays, speeches, introductions, and articles that reflect Gaiman’s thoughts on storytelling, literature, art, and his experiences as a writer. The book covers a broad spectrum of topics, from the importance of libraries to reflections on genre and creativity. It’s a rich, insightful look into Gaiman’s mind and his journey through the world of books and imagination.

Key Essays

  • Introduction: Some Things I Believe: A reflection on storytelling and the importance of fiction.
  • Why Our Future Depends on Libraries, Reading, and Daydreaming: A powerful speech about the essential role of libraries in society.
  • Make Good Art: Gaiman’s famous commencement address on creativity and persistence.
  • The Pornography of Genre: Gaiman’s thoughts on the boundaries between literary and genre fiction.
  • What the [Very] Bad Swear Word Is a Children’s Book Anyway?: A humorous take on the categorization of children’s literature.

Fun Fact

Gaiman delivered his famous "Make Good Art" speech at the University of the Arts in 2012, which quickly became viral, inspiring creators around the world.
