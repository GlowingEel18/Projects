Smoke & Mirrors

by Neil Gaiman

Overview

Smoke & Mirrors: Short Fictions and Illusions is a collection of short stories and poems by Neil Gaiman, first published in 1998. The book contains tales of fantasy, horror, and the surreal, showcasing Gaiman's ability to blend genres and play with the boundaries between reality and fiction. The stories explore themes such as myth, magic, and the dark corners of the human mind.

Notable Stories

  • Chivalry: A humorous story about an elderly woman who buys the Holy Grail in a secondhand shop.
  • The Price: A mysterious story about a stray black cat who protects a family from an unseen evil.
  • Shoggoth's Old Peculiar: A Lovecraftian tale with a comedic twist set in a seaside town.
  • The Goldfish Pool and Other Stories: A semi-autobiographical tale of Hollywood and its strange allure.
  • Murder Mysteries: A noir detective story set in the realms of Heaven, investigating a cosmic murder.

Fun Fact

The story "Chivalry" was Neil Gaiman's first published short story in a professional science fiction magazine and helped launch his short story career.
